# Newsletter Digest — 2026-03-12

## Top items
1. **Agents gain financial autonomy** – Ramp and Stripe now offer API-accessible virtual cards for AI agents, while Ramp’s AI Index shows Anthropic overtaking OpenAI in new business wins (70% share of first-time deployments).
2. **Vibe coding matures into enterprise-grade agent canvases** – Perplexity’s Computer for Enterprise/Personal Computer and Replit Agent 4 point to blended canvases, multi-agent orchestration, and multiplayer workflows as the next interface layer.
3. **AI-powered media bets escalate** – Netflix is reportedly ready to pay up to $600M for Ben Affleck’s InterPositive to streamline post-production without replacing creatives.

## Topic groups

### Agent operations & infrastructure
- **Ramp + Stripe agent credit cards**
  - *Key takeaways*: Secure agent-native spending via API/MCP/CLI (Ramp) and programmable limits with real-time risk scoring (Stripe). Preference for existing payment rails over crypto protocols underscores agent readiness for human rails.
  - *Action-worthy*: If you prototype autonomous workflows, study Ramp’s API guardrails and Stripe’s beta requirements to understand compliance expectations.
- **Anthropic becomes the enterprise favorite**
  - *Key takeaways*: Among Ramp customers, overall AI adoption is 47.6%; Anthropic commands 24.4% (rising) vs. OpenAI’s 34.4% (declining) and wins 70% of head-to-head evaluations.
  - *Action-worthy*: Track Anthropic features/support because enterprises you collaborate with may increasingly standardize on Claude.

### AI platforms & competition
- **OpenAI folding Sora into ChatGPT**
  - *Key takeaways*: Pivot from standalone Sora app toward boosting ChatGPT’s push to 1B weekly actives; aligns with Google’s native video generation in Gemini.
  - *Action-worthy*: Expect new multimodal assignments inside ChatGPT—plan experiments that mix text + video briefs once Sora access arrives.
- **Tesla & xAI’s Macrohard (Digital Optimus)**
  - *Key takeaways*: xAI computer-use agent processes rolling screen captures plus inputs; progress slowed by leadership turnover and annotation resets, hence Tesla’s heavier involvement.
  - *Action-worthy*: Useful case study for persistent-computer-use safety tradeoffs relevant to OpenClaw-style agents.
- **Lovable’s ARR spike & vibe coding market lift**
  - *Key takeaways*: Lovable jumped from $300M to $400M ARR in February; Cursor doubled to $2B ARR recently. Claude Code success appears to raise all boats.
  - *Action-worthy*: Benchmark your own workflow automation ideas against Lovable’s storytelling approach (focus on user outcomes, not “AI” branding).

### Agentic tooling & knowledge-work canvases
- **Perplexity Computer for Enterprise**
  - *Key takeaways*: Outcome-driven orchestration of agents/subagents with 400+ integrations; Slack-based collaboration; usage-based pricing acknowledges workload variance.
  - *Action-worthy*: Observe how persistent context + usage billing change team adoption; valuable for designing capstone projects.
- **Perplexity Personal Computer**
  - *Key takeaways*: Always-on agent on a Mac Mini with remote control and local file/app access. Security narrative targets users paying for turnkey OpenClaw setups.
  - *Action-worthy*: Evaluate whether offering curated local environments could be a service niche (but mind persistent-agent risks).
- **Replit Agent 4**
  - *Key takeaways*: Expanded canvas with direct manipulation, natural-language edits of subcomponents, parallel task queueing, and multiplayer co-building. Positions Replit as a full productivity suite.
  - *Action-worthy*: Try the multiplayer canvas for group coursework; note how Replit handles agent/human coordination for inspiration in your own tools.
- **Where vibe coding is heading**
  - *Key takeaways*: Interfaces are trending toward blended canvases, persistent context, multi-agent teams, and multiplayer collaboration. IDEs aren’t disappearing—they’re evolving upward.
  - *Action-worthy*: Frame your project ideas around these four design pillars to stay aligned with market direction.

### AI media & production
- **Netflix × InterPositive**
  - *Key takeaways*: Proposed $600M acquisition would deploy custom-trained models for editing tasks (lighting, reframing, background swaps) without generating new scenes—AI as a cost reducer that preserves creative control.
  - *Action-worthy*: For media-oriented assignments, emphasize AI assistance that respects artistic authorship—this is where budgets are flowing.

## Worth attention
- Review Ramp’s AI Index slides to understand enterprise vendor selection criteria—useful for pitching your own tools.
- Prepare lesson plans or demos that showcase blended canvases/multi-agent workflows; they map directly to where Replit and Perplexity are investing.
- Monitor Sora’s ChatGPT rollout timeline to anticipate multimodal homework or hackathon prompts.
